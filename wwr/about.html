History

White water rafting began with perilous 19th-century river expeditions, evolving from 1840s rubber raft inventions into a commercial sport by the 1940s-1960s. Pioneered by figures like Lt. John Fremont and popularised via military surplus boats, it grew rapidly, featuring in the 1972 Munich Olympics, and is now a popular global recreation sport.
